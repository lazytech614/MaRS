Projects

MaRS is a hub of innovation and creativity, where students come together to push the boundaries of technology and engineering. Through collaborative efforts and hands-on learning experiences, our club has embarked on a variety of exciting projects that showcase our passion for robotics.

Autonomous Delivery Robot

Design and build a small-scale autonomous robot capable of navigating through a college campus to deliver packages or items to designated locations. The robot should be equipped with sensors for obstacle detection, GPS for navigation, and a mechanism for securely carrying and delivering packages.

Smart Home Assistant Robot

Develop a robot that can assist users with various tasks within a home environment. The robot could be programmed to respond to voice commands, perform household chores such as cleaning or organizing, and provide information or reminders to users. Integration with smart home devices and artificial intelligence algorithms could enhance its functionality.

Robotic Arm with Computer Vision

Build a robotic arm equipped with a camera and computer vision algorithms to identify and manipulate objects in its environment. The project could involve training the robot to recognize specific objects, grasp them with precision, and perform tasks such as sorting or assembly based on visual cues.

Educational Robot for STEM Learning

Create an educational robot kit aimed at teaching fundamental concepts of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) to school students. The robot could be modular in design, allowing students to assemble and program it to perform various experiments and activities related to robotics, electronics, and coding.

Underwater Exploration Robot

Construct a remotely operated underwater vehicle (ROV) capable of exploring underwater environments such as lakes, rivers, or marine ecosystems. The ROV could be equipped with cameras, sensors, and manipulator arms to collect data, capture images, or retrieve samples for scientific research or environmental monitoring purposes.
